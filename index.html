01 — Why so few

We finish by hand because machines can't get bored, and boredom is where mistakes get caught.

Each bridge is hand-bevelled under a loupe before it's allowed near a movement. A finisher who rushes a chamfer will feel it under the loupe before we ever will — that discipline is the entire business model. There is no marketing budget. There has never been a marketing budget. The waitlist exists because four people can only finish so many movements between January and December, and we would rather disappoint you honestly than ship you something we wouldn't wear ourselves.

03 — How allocation works

Four steps. No exceptions for anyone, including people we like.

  1. 01

    Apply

    Tell us why you want the piece and what you'll wear it against. We read every application ourselves.

  2. 02

    Review

    Within 10 days, the atelier reviews the cycle's applications and shortlists against the remaining allocation.

  3. 03

    Offer

    If you're allocated a build slot, you have 14 days to accept and choose your dial colorway.

  4. 04

    Build & deliver

    Your movement is cased, regulated to your wrist's five positions, and shipped in 6–10 weeks.
